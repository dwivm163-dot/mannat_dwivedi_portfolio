Synesthetic Camera

Synesthetic Camera is an experimental camera that translates live audio into dynamic visuals. Instead of capturing a static image, the system generates shapes, colors, and motion in response to surrounding sound. Inspired by chromesthesia, the project reimagines the camera as a tool that captures both visual and sonic experience.

reflection

Design Process & Reflection

The initial concept was to generate an entirely new visual “landscape” from sound, using flowing lines and color fields to replace the camera image. Due to time and technical constraints in p5.js, this approach was simplified into overlaying graphics on top of a live camera feed.

The first prototype mapped sound mainly to pitch, which resulted in subtle and repetitive visuals. After feedback, the system shifted to using volume and changes in amplitude, along with distinct color thresholds and varied shape types. This made the visual responses more immediate, legible, and expressive.

This project shifted my approach from precise translation to expressive interpretation. Instead of mirroring sound directly, I focused on exaggeration and contrast to make differences more visible.

It also reframed the camera as a sensory tool, capturing not just what is seen, but how a moment feels through sound.
