GENERATIVE ART 006

Data Portrait

A portrait encoded entirely from personal data streams, visualizing habits and identity through code.

reflection

I tracked my music listening for a week and turned it into a spinning record. Each ring is one day of the week, going from the center outward: Monday → Sunday. The short colored dashes on a ring are individual listening sessions. The angle of each dash works like a clock: 12 o’clock at the top, moving clockwise through the day from morning to night. Colors show genre: blue = hip hop, green = alt, pink = pop, purple = R&B. I began with a numbered list of sessions for each day (time + genre). I sketched a few layouts and landed on a vinyl metaphor because circular time reads instantly like a clock and music already lives in the language of records. In code, I drew fixed guide rings, then placed arcs (short segments) at specific angles for each session. I added a start/legend screen so the reading instructions are clear, and a subtle spin only on the center stack to give the piece a sense of motion without making the data itself hard to read. Seeing my week as rings made patterns pop out. I tend to cluster listening at predictable times (commute / late evening), while mornings are lighter and shorter. What’s Gained / Lost in Visualization Gained: a quick, memorable snapshot of when and what I listen to, plus an easy day-by-day comparison. The circular clock mapping is intuitive and makes rhythm visible. Lost: context (where I was, who I was with, or why I chose a track) and duration detail per session—dashes are deliberately minimal to keep the graphic clean.
