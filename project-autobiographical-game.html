GENERATIVE ART 007

Autobiographical Game

A dreamlike mini-game where navigating the environment reflects personal narrative and self-exploration.

reflection

Reflecting on this assignment, it was definetly the most challenging one thus far, but also gave us the most creative freedom. Because we also had more that a week to work on the game, unlike previous assignment, I had more time to really be able to develop my idea and work on areas/aspects of game design I really enjoy like the visuals and aesthetics. There were some challenges in the process, which required me to rethink my intial design for the game. For instance, when I first started developing the idea for this game, I knew I wanted to incorporate some form of randomisation for the maze, so every time you play it doesnt just show the same maze layout again and again. My intial plan was to fake this randomisation by creating 3-4 different maze layouts, and coding the game to randomly alternate between the layouts without consecutive repition every time the player presses play. However, soon after greyboxing the game with the first intial maze layout, I realised I wouldnt have the time design and code for multiple different mazes, so alternatively, I chose a simpler option of randomizing the position of the objects in the game(stars, portals, and blackholes) instead of havung multiple different mazes.
